## üéØ Pilot Study Design (Nairobi, Summer 2026)

### Study Parameters

**Sample Size**: 100 participants
- 50 suspected PD patients (from KNH neurology clinic)
- 50 healthy controls (community screening)

**Location**: Nairobi informal settlements
- Kibera
- Mathare
- Mukuru

**Duration**: 6 weeks (June 15 - July 31, 2026)

**Team**:
- 1 UMich student (me)
- 1 KNH neurologist (clinical supervision)
- 10 Community Health Workers (recruited & trained)
- 1 local coordinator

---

### Week-by-Week Plan

**Week 1 (Jun 15-21): Setup & Training**
- Arrive in Nairobi
- Set up with UMich Kenya office
- Meet KNH partners
- CHW recruitment (10 CHWs from community clinics)

**Week 2 (Jun 22-28): CHW Training**
- Day 1-2: App training
- Day 3-4: Practice with healthy volunteers
- Day 5: Certification testing
- Day 6-7: Pilot run

**Week 3-4 (Jun 29 - Jul 12): Data Collection - Clinic**
- Screen 50 PD patients at KNH neurology clinic
- Neurologist performs standard UPDRS assessment
- Compare AI screening vs clinical diagnosis
- Collect feedback from clinicians

**Week 5 (Jul 13-19): Data Collection - Community**
- CHWs conduct 50 community screenings
- Focus on high-risk individuals (age 50+, family history)
- Referral protocol for high-risk cases to KNH

**Week 6 (Jul 20-26): Analysis & Debrief**
- Data analysis
- CHW feedback sessions
- Community feedback
- Final report to KNH
- Plan for continuation

## üìä Success Metrics

### Primary Outcomes

1. **Clinical Validation**:
   - Sensitivity: >85% (detect true PD cases)
   - Specificity: >80% (avoid false positives)
   - Agreement with neurologist diagnosis: >80%

2. **Operational Feasibility**:
   - CHW completion rate: >90% of screenings
   - App crash rate: <5%
   - Average screening time: <10 minutes
   - Battery usage: <10% per screening

3. **Community Acceptance**:
   - Participant satisfaction: >80% positive
   - CHW satisfaction: >90% would continue using
   - Community leader support: Endorsement letters

### Secondary Outcomes

1. **Multilingual Validation**:
   - Swahili model accuracy vs English baseline
   - Feature consistency across languages

2. **Referral Completion**:
   - % of high-risk cases that reach KNH
   - Time from screening to clinical evaluation

3. **Research Output**:
   - Journal paper submission
   - Conference presentation
   - Dataset contribution to research community

## üö® Risk Mitigation

### Potential Challenges

| Risk | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|---------------------|
| **IRB delays** | Apply early (March), have backup timeline |
| **Partner withdrawal** | Have 2-3 backup partners identified |
| **Community distrust** | Extensive pre-engagement, local champion |
| **Technology failure** | Offline-first design, backup devices |
| **Safety concerns** | Work with local CHWs, avoid risky areas |
| **COVID/health crisis** | Remote supervision option, flexible dates |
| **Funding shortfall** | Phase implementation, reduce sample size |

### Safety Protocols

1. **Personal Safety**:
   - Stay with UMich Kenya office recommendations
   - Local coordinator accompanies to communities
   - Check-in system with UMich (daily)
   - Travel insurance with emergency evacuation

2. **Data Security**:
   - Encrypted local storage only
   - No cloud upload
   - Password-protected devices
   - Secure physical storage of consent forms

3. **Participant Safety**:
   - Referral pathway for positive screens
   - Emergency contact for adverse events
   - Cultural sensitivity training for CHWs
